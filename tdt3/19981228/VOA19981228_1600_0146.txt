Israeli coalition and opposition lawmakers have agreed on May 17th 
as the date for early elections for a new Prime Minister and Parliament. 
A Labor Party spokesman said the date was a compromise between the 
opposition, which wanted April 27th elections and the right wing Likud 
Party which favored mid-June. The Labor spokesman said a second round 
run-off for Prime Minister, if needed, will be June 1st.
