Dr. George D. Crothers, the producer of the long-running CBS television 
religious program ``Lamp Unto My Feet,'' died on Nov. 27 in Sarasota, 
Fla., where he lived. He was 89. Crothers was born in Fort Morgan, 
Colo., and lived in Sarasota for 22 years. He graduated from Wooster 
College in Wooster, Ohio, and received his doctorate in modern European 
history from Columbia University. He taught at Columbia until 1946, 
when he joined CBS in New York, working for 30 years on public affairs 
programming. He was director of religious broadcasts at CBS and a 
producer of ``Lamp Unto My Feet,'' which ran on Sunday mornings from 
1948 to 1979. He is survived by his wife, Angelica; a son, Michael, 
of Congers, N.Y.; four grandchildren, and five great-grandchildren.
