More than 30 years after President Omar Bongo came to power, Gabonese 
voters were deciding Sunday whether the long-serving autocrat should 
be elected to another seven year term. There were scattered reports 
of stone-throwing and other violence in Libreville overnight, but 
voting was going calmly early Sunday morning at a sampling of the 
capital city's polling stations. No injuries were immediately reported 
from the overnight violence, and it was not clear what had sparked 
it. Soldiers were standing watch at all the city's polling stations 
Sunday. A number of polling stations had opened late, election monitors 
said, but there were no other reports of problems in the early hours 
of the voting. ``It went fine,'' said Jusline Bassalila, after voting 
in a working class Libreville neighborhood. Bongo, who ran a single 
party state from 1967-1990, is facing his second multi-party competition 
in the election. His opponents have claimed for weeks that the campaign
