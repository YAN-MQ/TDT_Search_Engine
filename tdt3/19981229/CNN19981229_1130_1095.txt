Bad weather in California may help cut, yes, cut, the cost of your 
morning glass of orange juice. Freezing temperatures devastated orange 
and other citrus crops last week. So growers, who normally sell most 
of their produce as fresh fruit, may be forced to squeeze juice from 
damaged oranges. The result -- lower prices for the juice at the checkout 
counter.
