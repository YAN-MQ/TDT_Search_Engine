Major tobacco companies led by market leader Phillip Morris upped 
wholesale prices of cigarettes by 45 cents a pack late yesterday and 
that is the biggest cigarette price increase ever. Analysts say the 
price hike will certainly be passed on to consumers and speculate 
that premium brands like Marlboro and camels could be saddled with 
even bigger increases. The increase comes on the heels of a $206 billion 
settlement with 46 states over Medicaid reimbursements. Shares of 
Phillip Morris which controls 53% of cigarette sales in the United 
States down.
