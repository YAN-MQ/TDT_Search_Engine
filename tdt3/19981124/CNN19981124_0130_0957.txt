Cigarette smokers soon will be experiencing financial fallout from 
a Multistate tobacco settlement. Monday Philip Morris raised wholesale 
prices of its brands $0.45 a pack. R.J. Reynolds has announced an 
identical increase, and other tobacco companies are expected to follow 
suit. An industry analyst says that means, on average, consumers will 
pay about $2.45 a pack, up from the current $1.95.
