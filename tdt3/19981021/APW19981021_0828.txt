The countdown ahead of the third and final test launch of Europe's 
Ariane 5 rocket was stopped Wednesday just as the launch window opened. 
Officials said the countdown for the unmanned rocket, set to blast 
off from its space base in South America, could be restarted at any 
moment. The launch window runs from 1600 to 1730 GMT. Technicians 
at the headquarters of Arianespace, in Evry south of Paris, gave no 
reason for the stop in the countdown. Arianespace commercializes the 
European rockets. The Ariane 5 is the most powerful in the series 
of unmanned European rockets. Wednesday's scheduled launch at the 
space center in French Guiana, on the northern shoulder of South America, 
is to be the third and final test launch before the rocket goes into 
commercial use. The first Ariane 5 launch crashed in June 1996 and 
the second launch a year ago was less than perfect. ``Flight 503,
