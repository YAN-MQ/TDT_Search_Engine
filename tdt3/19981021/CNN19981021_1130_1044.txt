A consumer products merger is making waves on Wall Street. Rubbermaid 
is being acquired by Newell company for $5.8 billion in stock and 
debt. Rubbermaid makes rubber and plastic storage containers, little 
tikes toys and graco strollers. It has had a tough time managing costs 
recently and has closed 11 factories in the last three years. The 
Lesser-known Newell company also makes some popular products including 
rolodex organizers and calphalon cookware. It has a reputation for 
its ability to manage costs. Now rubbermaid shares are up.
