Firefighters in southern California are getting some relief from slower 
winds and cooler temperatures. They are battling fires in Ventura 
and Santa Barbara counties. More than 9,000 acres have burned. The 
fire in Ventura county is 55% contained. The one in the Los padres 
national forest is 40% contained. Firefighters also are battling a 
third major wildfire in the mount waterman area, north of San Bernadino. 
It is reportedly being pushed by 30-mile-an-hour gusts.
