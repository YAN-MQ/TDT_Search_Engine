Microsoft is trying to paint a more favorable picture of the computer 
software company's relationship with its rival, Netscape. In the third 
day of the government's antitrust suit against Microsoft, defense 
lawyers produced copies of a 4-year-old E-mail from then Netscape 
chairman Jim Clark that was sent to Microsoft. It indicated the companies 
could work together and invited Microsoft to take a financial stake 
in Netscape. The government's case hinges on evidence Microsoft proposed 
to Netscape to illegally divide the Internet browser market.
