U.S. and Russian shuttle astronauts have entered the international 
space station for the first time. Orbiting 400 kilometers above the 
Earth. Commander Bob Kabana and Russian cosmonaut Sergei Pricolef 
left the Shuttle Endeavor and moved on to the U.S.-built Unity module 
of the space station. And turned on the lights. We have the hatch 
now coming open to Unity. Commander Bob Kabana and Sergei Pricolef 
entering the module together. The first astronauts aboard the international 
space station in orbit. A NASA spokesman calls the mission a milestone 
in space travel.
