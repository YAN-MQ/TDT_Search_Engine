President Clinton says the United States believes it is getting the 
necessary support from Gulf allies. As it steps up pressure on Iraq 
to stop obstructing U.N. weapons inspections. Correspondent David 
Gullis has this report from the White House. Defense Secretary William 
Cohen has been on a fast paced tour of the Gulf and the Middle East. 
To consult U.S. allies on the confrontation with Iraq. And although 
most governments in the region are publicly calling for a peaceful 
diplomatic solution to the latest crisis. Mr. Clinton told reporters 
here, there is ample support for an approach that includes the use 
of force, if necessary. To get Iraq to cooperate with the inspectors. 
We believe we'll have the support we need. And all options are on 
the table. Officials here say Mr. Clinton is sending his National 
Security Advisor Sandy Berger to Europe this coming weekend. To consult 
with U.S. allies there, on what are termed the "appropriate next steps 
in the Gulf crisis." David Gullis, VOA News, the White House.
