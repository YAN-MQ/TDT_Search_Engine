In an effort to further reduce costs, look for airplanes to become, 
well, a Little plainer. The chairman of united airlines is heading 
a drive to reduce the amount of customization that goes into producing 
an aircraft. As it stands now, each airline orders planes to its own 
specifications. But it's believed that each carrier could save at 
least 4% in airline costs if components became more standardized. 
So everything from paint schemes to coffee pots to clipboards is being 
reviewed. Lori Waffenschmidt, CNN, reporting.
