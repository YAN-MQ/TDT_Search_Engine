A Chinese company has signed a contract to supply the government with 
heavy machinery worth dlrs 10 million dollars for a hydropower project 
in central Myanmar, the state-run press reported Thursday. Under the 
contract, signed Wednesday, Yunnan Machinery and Equipment will provide 
machinery, equipment and spare parts for the construction of the Paunglaung 
underground hydropower station. The project will be able to generate 
280 megawatts when completed, said a report in the New Light of Myanmar. 
Last month, the Chinese company signed a dlrs 160 million dollar contract 
with the Myanmar Electric Power Enterprise, a state company. Under 
that deal, the company will provide machinery and equipment as well 
as the design of an underground tunnel. Myanmar is also called Burma.
