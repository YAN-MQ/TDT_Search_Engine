Moscow Mayor Yuri Luzhkov left for Japan on Thursday for talks with 
top government officials and executives, a news report said. Luzhkov, 
a likely leading contender in Russia's 2000 presidential elections, 
will meet Prime Minister Keizo Obuchi, Foreign Minister Masahiko Komura 
and Japanese businessmen, the ITAR-Tass news agency quoted Moscow 
municipal officials as saying. The visit is to last until Nov. 18. 
Obuchi was in Moscow on Thursday for talks with President Boris Yeltsin 
and other Russian officials.
