The International Football Federation's final rankings for 1998 have 
been released. And, Brazil remains the number one team, followed by 
France and Germany. The Brazilians finished almost five points ahead 
of world cup champion, France, in the final rankings. The French moved 
up four places from last year, to second. Croatia, which beat Germany 
in the quarterfinals of the World Cup, won the award for the best 
mover of the year, jumping 15 ranking places to fourth in the world. 
Argentina finishes the year, fifth., with Yugoslavia sixth, and Italy 
seventh. Two Asian teams made late charges to rise in the rankings. 
Japan jumped 10 places, from last year's 20th ranking in the world. 
Asians games champion, Iran, rose 17 places to number 27. The United 
States finished the year ranked 23rd.
