British engineering group General Electric Co. PLC announced twin 
deals Wednesday with other European businesses to create joint ventures 
in the missile and satellite industries. The deals mark the latest 
step in the long-running consolidation of the European defense and 
aerospace industries, aimed at creating European giants to rival the 
top defense groups in the United States. In the defense field, GEC 
announced a 50-50 joint venture with Italian defense engineering group 
Finmeccanica combining their ground, naval, missile system and air 
traffic control activities. The new company is to be called Alenia 
Marconi Systems. It will have turnover of about 1 billion pounds (dlrs 
1.7 billion) a year, they said, and will be the major supplier of 
missile systems outside the United States. In the space sector, the 
two have joined with France's Lagadere and Germany's Daimler-Chrysler 
Aerospace (Dasa) to merge satellite activities to form what they say 
will be Europe's top space venture. That will have turnover of about 
1.8 billion euros (dlrs 2.9 billion) a year and will be subject to 
approval by authorities in each of the countries involved as well
