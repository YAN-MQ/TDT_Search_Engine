South Carolina's attorney general says the state is dropping out of 
the microsoft antitrust lawsuit. He says America online's proposed 
acquisition of Netscape proves competition exists among Internet companies. 
In may, the federal government and 20 states filed the suit, claiming 
microsoft improperly used its dominance to box out competitors. So 
far South Carolina is the only state to pull out of the case. A spokesman 
for New York's attorney general describes South Carolina's role in 
the case as "minimal."
