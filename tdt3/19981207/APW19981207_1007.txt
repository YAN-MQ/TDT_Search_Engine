BRUSSELS, Belgium (AP) - Unemployment in the European Union stood 
at 9.8 percent in October, unchanged from September when it fell under 
10 percent for the first time in 6 years. Unemployment in the EU has 
dropped 0.8 percent since October 1997, when it stood at 10.6 percent, 
the EU's statistics office Eurostat said in a statement Monday. In 
the 11 countries that will join the single currency zone on Jan. 1, 
the jobless rate fell to 10.8 percent in October, from 10.9 in September, 
Eurostat said. Eurostat gives the following October figures for the 
15 EU countries - September figures in brackets: Denmark, 4.2 (4.3); 
Austria, 4.4 (4.5); Portugal, 4.5 (4.6); Sweden, 7.5 (7.6); Belgium, 
8.6 (8.8); Ireland, 8.6 (8.8); Germany, 9.3 (9.5); Finland, 11 (11.2); 
France, 11.8 (11.9); Spain, 18.2 (18.4). In Luxembourg and the Netherlands, 
September unemployment rates were respectively 2.2 percent and 3.7 
percent. No figures were given for October. No figures were available 
for Italy and Britain.
