Defending champion Vijay Singh received the top seed Tuesday for the 
World Match Play Championship with Tiger Woods, Ernie Els and Mark 
O'Meara claiming the next three seeded spots. Play begins Thursday 
at the Wentworth Club south of London with the eight non-seeded players 
facing off over 36 holes to reach the quarterfinals. The four seeds 
have byes Thursday. In Thursday play, Steve Stricker plays Patrick 
Sjoland with the winner facing Singh on Friday. Colin Montgomerie 
faces Thomas Bjorn with the winner going against O'Meara. In the other 
matchups, Lee Westwood is up against Stuart Appleby with the winner 
facing Els, and Darren Clarke plays Ian Woosnam with the winner playing 
Woods. Woosnam was named as a late replacement for Bernhard Langer, 
who was unable to play because of a chronic neck injury. Langer, himself, 
was named last week to replace American Stewart Cink when he withdrew.
