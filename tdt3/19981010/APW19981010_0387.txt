Intense-looking men huddle around a crackling battery-run shortwave 
radio, listening to the Albanian service of the Voice of America. 
Will NATO launch airstrikes against Yugoslavia over Kosovo? Will Yugoslav 
President Slobodan Milosevic back down? Will there be peace? These 
are the questions being asked. The question not being asked: When 
can we go home? ``I listen to Voice of America, Deutsche Welle, BBC, 
everything, all day, but peace will not come,'' Valci Zumer, a 28-year-old 
Kosovar refugee, says in a torrent of words.
