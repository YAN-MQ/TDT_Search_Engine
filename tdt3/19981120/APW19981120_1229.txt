An Israeli court on Friday extended by four days the detention of 
an Israeli man suspected of planning to attack Israeli Prime Minister 
Benjamin Netanyahu. Outside the magistrate's court in the northern 
town of Acre, several ultra-Orthodox Jews chanted and read psalms 
to show support for the suspect, Shabtai Bloch, 48, a member of the 
ultra-Orthodox Chabad group. Bloch allegedly plotted to attack Netanyahu 
during the prime minister's visit to the town of Safed in northern 
Israel on Thursday evening. Police gave no further details. Bloch's 
lawyer told Israel radio that his client opposes violence. Chabad 
members demonstrated against Netanyahu in Safed during the visit, 
angry over last month's Israel-Palestinian agreement that calls on 
Israel to hand over territory in the West Bank. Israel radio said 
Bloch organized the protest. At Chabad headquarters in New York, Rabbi 
Yehuda Krinsky, a spokesman for the movement, said ``Violence, even 
the threat of violence, is anathema'' to Chabad. Most Chabad members 
supported Netanyahu in Israel's 1996 election, but members now feel
