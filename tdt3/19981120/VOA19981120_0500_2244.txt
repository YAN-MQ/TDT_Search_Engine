Two moderate earthquakes have hit a rural area in southwestern China, 
injuring at least 300 people some of them seriously. The first quake, 
measuring 5.0 on the Richter scale, struck Hunan province Thursday 
evening. Minutes later, a second quake measuring 6.2 struck neighboring 
Sichuan province. An official with the seismology bureau in Hunan, 
says 63 people suffered a serious injuries, but there were no deaths.
