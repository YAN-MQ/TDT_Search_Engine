Some researchers are wondering about possible links between women 
who smoke and breast-feed and future smoking habits of their children. 
Researchers in Philadelphia studied five women. They found the samples 
of breast milk in smokers took on the smells of tobacco. The findings 
have led to speculation babies could grow accustomed to the taste 
and be more likely to smoke when they grow up.
