High-tech companies say it may take more than passwords to protect 
private information on the Internet. In fact, many companies at the 
comdex computer trade show in Las Vegas have been pushing "biometrics" 
as the next wave of network security. It uses small computer attachments 
to read eyes, fingerprints or other personal features that cannot 
be stolen, shared or forgotten. It's a $25 million-A-year industry. 
Industry analysts say that could expand to $1 billion by 2000.
