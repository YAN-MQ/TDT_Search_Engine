Key developments in the airstrikes against Iraq: ___ _U.S. and British 
warplanes launch a fourth wave of strikes on Iraq; A half-dozen loud 
blasts rocked the capital, indicating missile hits. _A defiant Iraq 
declared it would cut off all dealings with United Nations weapons 
inspectors. _In a radio address from the White House, President Clinton 
told listeners ``I believe the mission is going well.'' _At the Pentagon, 
Defense Secretary William Cohen told reporters the airstrikes had 
inflicted ``significant damage'' on Iraq's military systems. He said 
U.S. analysts believe the assault had set back Iraq's ballistic missile 
program by one year. _Iraqi vice president Taha Yassin Ramadan focused 
on civilian sites hit in the strikes, confirming earlier reports that 
telephone communications with southern provinces had been broken. 
He also said that the United States and Britain had hit an electricity 
plant and destroyed a factory that had been inspected by the UNSCOM 
weapons inspectors. _State-run Iraqi News Agency said five people 
died and 21 were wounded in a missile attack on the Al-Riyad neighborhood 
of the northern town of Kirkuk. The government said earlier that 25 
people were killed and 75 injured in the first two nights of attacks. 
_ A second U.S. aircraft carrier battle group _ led by the USS Carl 
Vinson _ moved into the Persian Gulf to join the USS Enterprise group, 
U.S. Navy officials said.
