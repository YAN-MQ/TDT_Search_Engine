Scientists have discovered fossils of what they believe is the world's 
oldest flower. The 142 million-year-old specimen was found in China 
and has a Woody stem and peapod-shaped fruit. Researchers say the 
fossil is from an evolutionary period when plants were first developing 
the flowering system. Today's common plant food sources came into 
existence after plants developed Flowers that attracted insects and 
other pollinators.
