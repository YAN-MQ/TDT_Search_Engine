Albania's election commission says voters have approved the country's 
first post-communist constitution. The commission today announced 
official results of Sunday's referendum. It said that 93.5 percent 
voted for the constitution that gives Albanians more religious freedom 
and more property rights. The voter turnout was just over 50 percent. 
Opposition leader, Sari Barisha has accused the government of manipulating 
the vote. And say that a low voter turnout means most Albanians reject 
the constitution. This is VOA News.
