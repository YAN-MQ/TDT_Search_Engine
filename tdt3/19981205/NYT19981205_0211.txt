George D. Crothers, a producer of the long-running CBS television 
program ``Lamp Unto My Feet'' and a director of religious broadcasting 
at the network, died Nov. 27 in Sarasota, Fla. He was 89. ``Lamp Unto 
My Feet'' focused on religious and cultural issues and was a fixture 
on CBS on Sunday mornings from 1948 to 1979. Born in Fort Morgan, 
Colo., Crothers graduated from Wooster College in Wooster, Ohio, and 
received a doctorate in modern European history from Columbia University. 
He taught at Columbia until 1946, when he joined the Columbia Broadcasting 
System, where he worked for 30 years in public affairs. Crothers was 
also moderator of the radio program ``Invitation to Learning.'' He 
is survived by his wife, Angelica; a son, Michael, of Congers, N.Y.; 
four grandchildren; and five great-grandchildren.
