TRIPOLI, Libya (AP) _ U.N. Secretary-General Kofi Annan failed Saturday 
to secure the handover of two Libyan suspects in the 1988 Pan Am bombing 
over Lockerbie, Scotland, despite what he called ``fruitful and positive'' 
talks with Moammar Gadhafi. Annan said, however, he believed an arrangement 
for trying the two suspects could be secured in ``not an inordinate'' 
amount of time. The secretary-general spoke to reporters after meeting 
the Libyan leader at an undisclosed location in the Libyan desert. 
Libyan TV showed Gadhafi, in a brown robe and cap, shaking hands with 
Annan. It was a long day of diplomacy for the 60-year-old Annan. He 
left Jerba, Tunisia, in the morning for Tripoli, held talks with Libyan 
officials in the coastal city of Sirte, then took what was said to 
be a two-hour journey to his desert meeting with Gadhafi. Annan is 
trying to get Libya to go along with a U.S.-British plan to try the 
two suspects before a panel of Scottish judges in the Netherlands 
for the Dec. 21, 1988, bombing of an airliner over Lockerbie that
