It's been nearly 17 years since Bob Marley died, and now some new 
music from the master of reggae is about to released. Twenty-three 
songs were discovered recently, and archivists say that these songs 
may be some of the best work the young Jamaican ever produced. The 
material was recorded during Marley's prime musical years of 1967 
to 1972. His music still remains a huge influence in R&B and the Motown 
sound today. It was not announced exactly when the album would be 
released, but it's expected to be early in 1999. I'm Catherine Kohl.
