Georgianna Morris Crowther Kappes, a longtime teacher of history in 
private schools, died Oct. 17 at Greenwich Hospital in Greenwich, 
Conn. She was 94 and lived in Greenwich. Mrs. Kappes, known as Nancy, 
was born in Lutherville, Md., and graduated from National Cathedral 
School in Washington and Smith College. She returned to school to 
earn a master's degree at Columbia University Teachers College in 
1953 and taught at preparatory schools until retiring from the Masters 
School in Dobbs Ferry, N.Y., in the late 1960s. Her husband, Carl, 
died in 1962. She was a sister of the late Bosley Crowther, a film 
critic for The New York Times. She is survived by a son, Carl Kappes 
III of Greenwich; two daughters, Ellen Kappes of Northeast Harbor, 
Maine, and Karen Kappes of Holden, Mass., and four grandsons.
