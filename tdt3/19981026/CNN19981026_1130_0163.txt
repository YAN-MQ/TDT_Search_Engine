Some members of Congress are concerned about the role the CIA will 
play in the Middle East peace process. A Senate committee will hold 
hearings on one part of the agreement, which calls for the CIA to 
monitor Palestinian anti-terrorist efforts. Secretary of state Madeleine 
Albright says such work is nothing new for the agency, because it 
has been fighting terrorism in that region the past two years. But 
senator Richard Shelby, chairman of the intelligence committee, says 
he's troubled the agency's role will be so visible.
