BRUSSELS, Belgium (AP) - The European Union on Wednesday approved 
1.5 million European currency units (dlrs 1.7 million) in humanitarian 
aid for Bulgaria to help the needy through the winter. In a statement, 
the EU's humanitarian office said the money would ``help those hardest 
hit by stringent economic reform measures.'' The funds will help provide 
food and medicine to individuals as well as clinics, hospitals and 
social institutions. The aid package will also contribute to the running 
of soup kitchens in 15 regions throughout Bulgaria. The EU will be 
channel the funds through humanitarian organizations over the next 
nine months. Bulgaria is one of 10 former-communist nations in eastern 
Europe lobbying to join the EU membership next decade.
