For The World, this is Pamela McCall in the BBC Newsroom in London. 
The NATO alliance has decided not to carry out immediate air strikes 
against Yugoslavia. The announcement follows a substantial withdrawal 
of Serbian troops from Kosovo. President Clinton welcomed the move, 
but warns NATO is ready to act, if Yugoslav President Slobodon Milosevic 
steps out of line. NATO's willingness to act, combined with determined 
diplomacy created this chance to end the suffering and the repression 
in Kosovo and to put its people on the path to peace. But this is 
a chance, not a guarantee. That is why NATO, today, agreed to retain 
the authority, the forces and the readiness to act, if Mr. Milosevic 
backslides on his commitments. President Clinton.
