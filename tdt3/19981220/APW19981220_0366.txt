Famous Indian astrologer, B.V. Raman, who promoted scientific study 
of the subject, died Sunday at the 87, his family said. Raman was 
editing his Astrological Magazine when he died of a heart attack in 
the southern city of Bangalore, his family told local news agencies. 
He is survived by his wife, five sons and two daughters. Raman founded 
dozens of astrology study groups across the world and wrote more than 
100 books on the subject.
