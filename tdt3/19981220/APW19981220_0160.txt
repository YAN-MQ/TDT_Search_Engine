KUALA LUMPUR, Malaysia (AP) - Malaysia's premier cycling race, Le 
Tour de Langkawi, has attracted 20 entries so far and tour executive 
chairman Wan Lokman Wan Ibrahim said Sunday that organizers hope to 
have 25 participants by the closing date on Dec 31. All 20 teams have 
confirmed participation in the race which will be held from February 
3 to Feb. 14. One of the teams is newcomer Acceptcard Pro Cycling 
of Denmark, which is among six professional teams that have signed 
up so far. Others include Italians outfits Mapei Bricobi and Scrigno 
Gaerna, Saturn of the United States and MROZ of Poland. The grueling 
1,773.5 km race will begin on a resort island on the west coast of 
peninsular Malaysia and finish in the capital of Kuala Lumpur. Wan 
Lokman said 14 national teams have confirmed participation including 
Malaysia, the Philippines, Kazakstan, Australia, New Zealand, Britain, 
Denmark and Canada. Japan, South Korea and China, who were absent 
in the previous race, were also scheduled to participate, he said. 
Wan Lokman said the competition, offering 1.5 million ringgit (dlrs 
375,000) in prize money, was expected to feature 150 racers.
