Italy's Patrick Holzer unexpectedly led the first run of Sunday's 
World Cup giant slalom, ahead of Swiss Michael Von Gruenigen and Austrians 
Andreas Schifferer and Hermann Maier. Holzer, 28, whose only World 
Cup victory dates back to 1992, skied down the icy Gran Risa course 
in the fastest time of one minute, 18.42 seconds. Von Grueningen, 
the 1997 World giant slalom champion, was 0.14 seconds behind. Schifferer 
and defending World Cup champion Maier trailed the Italian skier by 
0.71 and 1.03 seconds respectively. Top-10 first-run finishers: 1, 
Patrick Holzer, Italy, 1:18.42. 2, Michael Von Gruenigen, Switzerland, 
1:18.56. 3, Andreas Schifferer, Austria, 1:19.13. 4, Hermann Maier, 
Austria, 1:19.45. 5, (tie) Rainer Salzgeber, Austria, 1:19.68. -- 
Stve Locher, Switzerland, 1:19.68. 7, Stephan Eberharter, Austria, 
1:20.03. 8, Christian Mayer, Austria, 1:20.39. 9, (tie) Lasse Kjus, 
Norway, 1:20.46. -- Fredrik Nyberg, Sweden, 1:20.46.
