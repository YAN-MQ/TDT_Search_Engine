India says it remains committed to bringing in to force a global nuclear 
test ban treaty but will not negotiate away it's nuclear weapons capability. 
Indian Prime Minister Atal Bihari Vajpayee says his government is 
determined to have what he called a minimum credible nuclear deterrent, 
but he says India is prepared to work for the implementation of an 
international ban on nuclear weapons testing by September of next 
year. He was responding to questions in the lower house of parliament 
about progress in talks with the United States on India's nuclear
