Consumers kept up their spending in September even though personal 
incomes grew only slightly. The result was a raid on savings accounts. 
The Commerce Department said that incomes grew by only 2/10 of a percent 
while spending grew by 5/10 of a percent. That plunged the savings 
rate into negative territory for the first time since 1959.
