Candidates in the nation's most populous state also spent the day 
trying to get their core supporters to the polling booth tomorrow. 
NPR's Richard Gonzalez reports from San Francisco. The California 
gubernatorial and Senate races are among the most closely watched 
contests in the country. Democratic Lieutenant Governor Gray Davis 
holds a strong lead over Republican Attorney General Dan Lundgren 
in the governor's race. Democratic Senator Barbara Boxer faces a stiff 
challenge from Republican State Treasurer Matt Fawn. The Democrats, 
Davis and Boxer, are flying around the state today for get out the 
vote rallies in Sacramento, Los Angeles and San Francisco. The Republicans, 
Lundgren and Fawn, will appear in appear in Sacramento, Fresno and 
San Diego. There's some good news for Democrats. A new field poll 
shows that 65 percent of California voters approve of President Clinton's 
job performance and 80 percent said the Clinton sexual scandal will 
not affect their votes in congressional races. Richard Gonzalez, NPR 
news, San Francisco.
