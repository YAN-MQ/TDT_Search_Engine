A five-member delegation from football's world governing body, FEFA, 
has arrived in Seoul, South Korea to inspect preparations for the 
2002 World Cup. The delegation led by FEFA Vice President Antonio 
Martariz arrived on Monday. They plan to visit sites in Seoul and 
several other cities where South Korea plans to build stadiums to 
host the first World Cup ever held in Asia. Japan is co-hosting the 
event and the delegation will begin its tour of venues there on Friday. 
Depending on what the delegation finds, FEFA plans to announce the 
tournament host cities for next May.
