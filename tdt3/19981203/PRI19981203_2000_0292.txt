Hindu extremist in India are attacking cinemas showing a controversial 
film called "Fire", which centers on a lesbian relationship. Members 
of the Shift Sena organization today attacked a movie theater in Delhi. 
Yesterday, the vandalized two cinemas in Bombay. Daniel Lack reports 
from Delhi. "Fire" does tell provocative story. Two sisters-in-law 
trapped in loveless marriages develop an emotional and eventually 
physical relationship. But, it's been praised by critics all over 
the world. Police in Delhi say they have begun making arrest and they'll 
prevent further attacks. But, cinema owners aren't taking any chances. 
Some have stopped showing the film in fear of further violence. Filmmakers 
in Bombay, India's cinema capital, have condemned what they call "cultural 
terrorism" and called for protection from the authorities. Daniel 
Lack, BBC, Delhi.
