German motorists got good news and bad news in the coalition deal 
worked out by the incoming center-left government: no autobahn speed 
limits, but higher gasoline taxes. After two weeks of talks, the newly 
elected Social Democrats and Greens wrapped up their pact Saturday 
with agreement on transportation policy and energy taxes _ among the 
most contentious issues. ``We're done,'' Social Democratic party chairman 
Oskar Lafontaine said when asked what remained to be settled. On Sunday, 
both parties announced details on social welfare policy, including 
a pledge to scrap cuts in retirement benefits pushed through by outgoing 
Chancellor Helmut Kohl. The new administration gave itself two years 
to overhaul the retirement system, strained by Germany's aging population.
