Finally, we humans share about 98 and half percent of our DNA with 
chimps. To which the noted physiologist Jared Diamond said we are 
the third chimpanzee. Well, the journal Science quotes Canadian anthropologist 
Peter Frost, that we may be closer that we think, genetically, to 
many species. Looking at a common cell protein, Prof. Frost said all 
104 of its amino acids, or building blocks, are identical in humans 
and chimps. We and donkeys share 88 out of 104 of these amino acids, 
and 87 out of 104 with a duck. So, be kind to our web-footed friends. 
That duck may be somebody's uncle. Well, that's it for today. I'm 
Brian Sislack, reminding you to join me next time for another edition 
of Science World.
