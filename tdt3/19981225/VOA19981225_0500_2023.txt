The British Air Force has begun evacuating foreigners from threatened 
violence in the Sierra Leonian capitol, Freetown. Eighty-one foreigners 
were flown out Thursday, on two military transports that were called 
in. After fighting around Freetown intensified between rebel forces 
and troops supporting President Achmed Tahan Kaba. The United States 
had respondent to the crisis by suspending operations in its Freetown 
embassy. And ordering U.S. government personnel to leave. The U.N. 
observer mission is also removing non-essential staff from the country. 
West African peace-keepers and thousands of militia loyal to President 
Kaba have been shifted to Freetown to tighten security after a rebel 
attack earlier this week, 30 kilometers from the capitol.
