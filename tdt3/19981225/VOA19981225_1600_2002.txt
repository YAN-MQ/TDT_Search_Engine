Iraq's official newspaper says Baghdad might agree to have its weapons 
programs electronically monitored if U.N. sanctions are lifted first. 
The newspaper, which reflects the party of the ruling Baath Party, 
said in an editorial Friday: After sanctions are lifted, Iraq could 
discuss continued surveillance by cameras and other means.
