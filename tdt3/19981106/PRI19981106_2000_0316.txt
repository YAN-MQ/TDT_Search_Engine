Senator Daniel Patrick Moynihan says he plans to retire at the end 
of his current term in 2000. Moynihan, a New York Democrat, had served 
as chairman of the Senate Finance Committee, and he's widely regarded 
as knowledgeable on foreign policy, Social Security, and welfare issues. 
Before running for Congress, he was ambassador to the United Nations. 
Democrats mentioned his possible successors to Moynihan are Housing 
Secretary Andrew Cuomo and Robert Kennedy, Jr.
