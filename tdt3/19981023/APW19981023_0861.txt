As many as 37 people were feared drowned when an overcrowded row boat 
capsized Friday in a fast flowing river in northern India, police 
said. The boat, which had a maximum capacity of 40 people was carrying 
64 when it sank in the Bagad River in the northern Uttar Pradesh state, 
police said. Divers rescued 27 passengers from the river, and the 
bodies of 10 victims. Twenty-seven people, mostly women and children, 
were still missing. Rescue workers were still searching for survivors 
and bodies, police said.
