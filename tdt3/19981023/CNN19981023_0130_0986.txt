Seven manufacturers of Diesel truck engines have reached a $1 billion 
settlement with the environmental protection agency. The companies 
were accused of selling engines designed to fool government clean 
air regulators. The engines passed emissions tests in the laboratory, 
but would shut off pollution control equipment when they were operated 
on the road. Manufacturers sold about 1.3 million of the engines, 
accounting they say, for about 6% of the vehicle pollution in the 
U.S.
