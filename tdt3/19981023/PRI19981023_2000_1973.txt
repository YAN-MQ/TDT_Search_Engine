Ecuador and Peru have agreed to sign a peace treaty to end their long 
running border dispute. They agreed to abide by a settlement proposed 
by the four countries brought into resolve the issue: Chile, Argentina, 
Brazil, and the United States. It establishes the frontier between 
Ecuador and Peru along the summit of the Condor Mountains. Three years 
ago, the two countries fought a brief war over the disputed land. 
The agreement is to be signed on Monday in the Brazilian capital of 
Brazilia. Those are the news headlines from the BBC Newsroom in London.
