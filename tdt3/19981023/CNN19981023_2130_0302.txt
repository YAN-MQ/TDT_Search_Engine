Texas governor George W. bush is telling residents of flooded areas 
of texas, there's a brighter day coming. Today, bush got a first-hand 
look at damage from flooding that has buried the towns of Victoria 
and Cuero under water. He surveyed the scene from the air and on the 
ground, accompanied by FEMA director James Lee witt. Flooding and 
tornadoes in central and south Texas have killed 29 people and have 
done $500 million worth of damage. Witt added five more counties to 
the 20 eligible for federal aid.
