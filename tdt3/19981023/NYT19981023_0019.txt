John Kifner, a reporter for The New York Times, received the 1998 
John Chancellor Award for Excellence in Journalism at a dinner in 
Philadelphia on Wednesday night. Kifner, 56, now a metropolitan reporter 
for The Times, won the award for foreign and domestic reporting over 
the course of his 30-year-career, which has included coverage of the 
Iranian revolution, the Persian Gulf war, as well the 1968 Democratic 
convention. The award is given in honor of John Chancellor, the NBC 
television correspondent and anchor who died in 1996. It carries a 
$25,000 prize.
