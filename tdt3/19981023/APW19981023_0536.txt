Three U.N. peacekeeping soldiers from Nepal were injured Friday when 
their jeep flipped over in a Lebanese village. A U.N. ambulance rushing 
to the site overturned on the way, injuring the driver, also a Nepalese, 
officials said The four soldiers from the United Nations Interim Force 
in Lebanon, or UNIFIL, were taken to hospital in a second ambulance, 
the official who spoke on condition of anonymity. The soldiers who 
suffered light to moderate injuries were in stable condition, he said. 
No other details were available. The first accident occurred in the 
village of Jbal al-Botom, around 10 kilometers (six miles) southeast 
of this southern port city. A total of 222 peacekeepers have been 
killed since UNIFIL was deployed in South Lebanon in March 1978 after 
a brief Israeli incursion. Nine nations contribute to the 4,500-member 
UNIFIL force. They are Finland, Italy, France, Poland, Norway, Ghana, 
Ireland, Nepal and Fiji. An Indian contingent is expected to replace 
the Norwegian troops within the next few months.
