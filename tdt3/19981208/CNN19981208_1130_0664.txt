African-Americans are the fastest growing AIDS population in the U.S., 
making up 43% of new cases. Although the road to HIV is paved with 
intravenous drug use and unprotected sex, these issues have been taboo 
subjects in the African-American culture. We have a code of silence, 
and we just don't like to talk about those things. And the reality 
is -- silence equals death. Leaders say access to health care may 
not be as big an issue as trust in the health system, sticking to 
a medical regimen and changing bad habits. They hope federal dollars 
for education, prevention and treatment will be part of the cure. 
In October, President Clinton announced a $156 million program to 
combat aIDS among blacks and other minorities. For the latest health
