Four men who worked for a British telecommunications company in Chichnia 
have been found dead. From London, NRP's Michael Goldfarb reports. 
The four, three Britons and a New Zealander, were kidnapped October 
3 in the Chitin capital, Grosney. Their severed heads were found in 
the countryside outside the city. No bodies have been found. The men 
were working for Granger Telecom. Helping to install equipment to 
allow mobile phone communications in Chichnia which is still struggling 
to rebuild after its 1994-96 war with Russia. Since the start of this 
year, 176 people have been kidnapped in the region, 90 have been released. 
Britain's foreign office warns all Britons to stay out of the country. 
In a statement issued after the heads were found, foreign Secretary 
Robin Cook, reiterated that warning. He also demanded to know of the 
Russian government what was being done to bring to justice those who 
committed such repugnant murders. Michael Goldfarb, NPR News, London.
