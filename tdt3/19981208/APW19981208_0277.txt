Tomohiro Iizuka batted in two runs Tuesday as Japan beat Taiwan 4-1 
in a first-round game of the Asian Games baseball tournament. China 
trounced the Philippines 17-7 in the day's other game. Although Taiwan 
outhit the Japanese 10-8, Japan scored three runs in the second inning 
and one in the fifth, after Pan Chung-wei's homer gave Taiwan its 
lone run in the fourth. It was Japan's first game. Taiwan had lost 
16-5 to South Korea on Monday. For China, also playing its first game, 
Luo Yubin drove in four runs and Lin Xiaofan, with three singles in 
four at-bats, batted in three. Because of China's 10-run lead, the 
game was stopped after eight innings. The Chinese led 9-2 after three 
thanks to a seven-run third. For the Philippines (0-2), Lorenzo Icban 
and Robert Luliesco each drove in two runs.
