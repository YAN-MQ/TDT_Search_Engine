Donald Gerhard Brinkmann, a partner in the New York office of Gensler, 
an international architecture and interior planning firm, died on 
Dec. 19 at New York University Hospital. He was 53 and lived in Manhattan. 
The cause was heart failure after a stroke, the firm reported. Brinkmann, 
a native of Riverside, Calif., attended California State Polytechnic 
University and graduated with a degree in architecture in 1969 from 
the University of California at Berkeley. He then spent 13 years as 
a designer and associate in the San Francisco office of Skidmore, 
Owings &AMP; Merrill, architects. He moved to New York in 1982 to 
join Gensler Architecture Design and Planning Worldwide and was named 
a partner in 1985. As the lead designer in the office, he worked on 
projects in San Francisco, Minneapolis, Buffalo, Miami, Washington, 
New York, London and South America. Brinkmann designed office space 
for law firms, financial institutions, corporations and consulting 
firms. He recently completed projects for Baron Capital in New York, 
QVC Network headquarters in West Chester, Pa., and Swiss Re American 
Corp. offices in New York. He was also working on designs for Christie's 
auction house in New York and Bank Boston. He was elected to the Interior 
Design Hall of Fame in 1991. He is survived by a brother, John, of 
Riverside, and his companion, Dr. Mark Weinrod.
