The Kazak government plans to create a fleet of tankers to ship oil 
abroad, the ITAR-Tass news agency reported Saturday. The government 
will spend dlrs 94 million on purchasing tankers from other countries 
or construction of the ships at its own Zenit defense plant in Uralsk, 
northwestern Kazakstan. The tankers will ply the Volga River, Black 
Sea, Bosporus Strait and the Mediterranean, ITAR-Tass said.
