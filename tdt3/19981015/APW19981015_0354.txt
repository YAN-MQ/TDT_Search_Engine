Italian Premier Romano Prodi on Thursday gave up on his bid to form 
a new government, informing President Oscar Luigi Scalfaro that his 
negotiations had failed. ``I believe it was my duty to take up Scalfaro's 
invitation to form a new government,'' Prodi told reporters after 
their meeting. ``I knew the difficulties I would face, but in the 
best interests of my country, I needed to take risks and face a difficult 
situation. But my assignment proved impossible.'' Scalfaro can now 
ask someone else to try to form a government or he can call early 
elections. Most observers think elections are unlikely. ``I hope Italy 
will soon have a stable government and a cohesive majority,'' Prodi
