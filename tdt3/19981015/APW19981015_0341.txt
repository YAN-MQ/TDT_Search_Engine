A trade official in the Ural Mountains city of Chelyabinsk was charged 
with bribery after demanding dlrs 800 from a businessman trying to 
open an electronics store, a news agency said Thursday. Mark Zlotver, 
chairman of a district trade committee in Chelyabinsk, was arrested 
after accepting the bribe that was paid in specially marked bills, 
ITAR-Tass said. It said the transaction was videotaped by a hidden 
camera. It said the businessman, who wanted to sell audio and video 
equipment, first agreed to pay the bribe, but then reported Zlotver 
to police who set up the sting. Corruption is a widespread problem 
in Russia as it tries to move to a free-market economy.
