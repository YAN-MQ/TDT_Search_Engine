Heavyweight boxing champions Evander Holyfield and Lenox Lewis have 
verbally agreed to meet in a title unification bout, the deal will 
become official when the two fighters sign a formal agreement. The 
bout is expected to take place in Las Vegas, Nevada in February or 
March. The event was brokered by promoter Don King and Lewis's manager 
Frank Malone in London, earlier this week. Holyfield holds the World 
Boxing Association and International Federation Crowns, while Lewis 
is wearing the World Boxing Federation Belt. Holyfield is expected 
to earn more than 20 million dollars from the bout, while Lewis will 
take home about 8 million dollars.
