If you're at low risk for heart disease when you're middle-aged, chances 
are you'll spend fewer Medicare dollars on hospital services when 
you get older. That's according to a study in today's "New England 
journal of medicine." Researchers say the most savings were associated 
with heart disease risk factors that are preventable -- smoking, high 
blood pressure and high cholesterol.
