Malaysian news reports say prosecutor are ready to move forward with 
all 10 charges against former Deputy Prime Minister Anwar Ibrahim. 
The "News Street Times" quotes Attorney General Motar Abdullah as 
saying the prosecution side has applied to the high court to set dates 
for hearings. Mr. Anwar is expected to face the first 4 charges against 
him beginning November 2nd. Mr. Motar says that if hearings on the 
first 4 charges can be completed in November, the prosecution can 
proceed with the remaining charges in December and January. The former 
Deputy Prime Minister has pleaded not guilty to 5 charges of corruption 
and 5 charges of sodomy.
