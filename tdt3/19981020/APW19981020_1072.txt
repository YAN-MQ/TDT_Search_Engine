Fourth-seed Yevgeny Kafelnikov of Russia beat Australia's Richard 
Fromberg 7-6 (7-4), 6-3 Tuesday to advance into the second round of 
the IPB Czech Indoor tennis tournament. Slovakia's Jan Kroslak upset 
fifth-seeded Croatian Goran Ivanisevic 4-6, 6-2, 7-6 (7-4) and Germany's 
David Prinosil ousted sixth seed Mark Philippoussis of Australia 6-3, 
6-7 (8-10), 6-3. In other first round action, Sweden's Nicklas Kulti 
overpowered Jan-Michael Gambill of the United States 3-6, 6-3, 6-2 
and Bohdan Ulihrach of the Czech Republic beat Germany's Lars Rehmann 
6-4, 6-3. Kafelnikov and Fromberg held serve throughout the first 
set, and the Russian favorite prevailed in the tiebreaker 7-4 with 
excellent returns and passing shots. Kafelnikov then broke Fromberg 
in the fourth game of the second set to take a 3-1 lead and controlled 
the match from then on. Second-seed Andre Agassi of the United States 
is scheduled to play Denmark's Kenneth Carlsen on Wednesday.
