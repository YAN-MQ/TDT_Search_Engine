A growing number of people are trying to find medical information 
online. But an article in "the New England journal of medicine" found 
less than 2% of physicians offer digital access to patients. Researchers 
are discussing the role of online services in the medical world at 
a conference today, at Duke University. Some say anonymous E-mail 
consultations are less intimidating than direct contact with their 
physicians, but others are concerned about liability for physicians 
online.
