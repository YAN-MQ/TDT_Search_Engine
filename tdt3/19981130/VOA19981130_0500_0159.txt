A Malaysian appeals court has temporarily suspended the three-month 
jail sentence give to a lawyer representing former Deputy Prime Minister 
Anwar Ibrahim. Defense officials say the stay will allow time for 
an appeal of the ruling imposed earlier today by the judge presiding 
over Mr. Anwar's trial. Defense lawyer Zaineer Zachariah was given 
the sentence for contempt after he refused to apologize for accusing 
prosecutors of fabricating evidence. Reporter Lyn Shu Ling describes 
just how big this case is in Malaysia It's a very big case involving 
a lot of personalities very high up in society, very powerful personalities. 
And involving charges which are very, very sensational. Reporter, 
Lyn Shu Ling.
