Intel wants to make sure there's a faster computer memory chip for 
the new millennium. The world's largest computer chip maker is investing 
$500 million in Micron technology, a top American manufacturer of 
memory chips. In return, Intel will get a supply of chips and the 
right to buy stock in Micron. It's part of Intel's strategy to assure 
it has the best technology to develop faster computers in the future.
