Marcelo Rios of Chile recovered from a first-set loss to Lleyton Hewitt 
to beat the Australian teen-ager 5-7, 6-3, 6-4, on Friday and reach 
the semifinals of the Singapore Open. He will face America's Jim Courier 
in Saturday semifinals. Courier dispatched countrman Jan-Michael Gambill 
6-4, 6-4. Hewitt, 17, jumped ahead of of the 22-year-old and third-ranked 
Rios, but the South American rallied to claim the match in one hour 
and 57 minutes. Australia's Mark Woodforde, who earlier eliminated 
No. 2 seed Goran Ivanisevic from the tournament, moved into the semifinals 
with a 4-6, 6-2, 6-3 victory over of Denmark's Kenneth Carlsen. In 
the Saturday semifinals, Woodforde will face the Sjeng Schalken of 
the Netherlands, who beat his countryman John van Lottum 3-6, 6-3, 
6-4.
