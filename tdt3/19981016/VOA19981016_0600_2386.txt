Senior diplomats from India and Pakistan has begun peace talks in 
Islamabad. VOA Correspondent, Douglas Faxton has more from the Pakistani 
Capital. Pakistani Foreign Secretary, Sham Shad Ahmed and his Indian 
counterpart Kara Gunad met at the Punjab house mansion in Islamabad. 
Speaking to reporters just before the talks, Mr. Ahmed said matters 
of peace and security are on the agenda and this could include nuclear 
issues. The need to revert the risk of conflict. The need to develop 
a regime of neutral and the need to prevent a. Indian Pakistan conducted 
nuclear tests in May prompting international concerns over increased 
tensions in South Asia. Indian Foreign Secretary Raguna for his part, 
told reporters only that the details of the talks would be revealed 
later. The negotiations have resumed after a one year standoff. Douglas 
Faxton, VOA News, Islamabad.
