An international envoy on Friday denounced Yugoslav authorities for 
closing down three independent dailies and two radio stations critical 
of the regime. ``I asked foreign minister Zivadin Jovanic about the 
reasons of such undemocratic measure,'' said the head of the 54-nation 
Organization for Security and Cooperation in Europe, Bronislaw Geremek. 
``The answer that I obtained was that the situation was exceptional 
and this measure concerns only this exceptional situation,'' said 
Geremek, who was in Belgrade to sign a pact providing for OSCE's surveillance 
of strife-torn Kosovo province. On Wednesday, Serbian police closed 
the major independent Nasa Borba daily. Late Tuesday, two other newspapers 
_ Dnevni Telegraf and Danas _ were banned. The authorities said their 
recent reports on NATO threats against Yugoslavia violated a broad, 
vaguely worded ban on unpatriotic behavior. Members of Nasa Borba's 
editorial staff said at a news conference Friday that they would appeal 
against the closure. But their lawyer noted that no media have ever 
won a case against the state. ``The attacks and repression against 
independent media are only a part of the overall pressure on freedom 
and critical thought in this country,'' said Novak Pribicevic, an
