The U.N. Security Council held an emergency meeting on Iraq Wednesday 
while Secretary-General Kofi Annan appealed to Iraqi leader Saddam 
Hussein to immediately resume cooperation with U.N. weapons inspectors. 
Russia requested the council meeting after 233 U.N. personnel were 
pulled out of Iraq because of the possibility of airstrikes by the 
United States to compel Baghdad to permit the U.N.-mandated inspections 
that monitor Iraq for weapons of mass destruction. The Security Council 
is sharply divided over the use of force against Iraq. The United 
States and Britain maintain that they can take military action, based 
on previous U.N. resolutions. Russia and China insist that fresh council 
approval is needed. British Ambassador Jeremy Greenstock said that 
while diplomacy remains active, a military solution is now ``a serious 
option.'' But Russian Ambassador Sergey Lavrov said ``the use of force 
is fraught with very serious consequences not only for the U.N. ability 
to continue to monitor inside Iraq but also for the stability in the 
region and for the Middle East in general.'' Moscow has been in contact 
with Baghdad trying to get the Iraqis to resume cooperation and resolve 
the situation peacefully, which he stressed was the only real solution.
