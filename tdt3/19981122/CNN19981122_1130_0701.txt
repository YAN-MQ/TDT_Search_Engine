Nearly a decade after the Berlin wall came down, there's word the 
CIA reaped a bonanza of secret papers from east Germany. "The Washington 
Post" reports the U.S. received a complete set of spy records from 
east Germany's secret police. The files reportedly contain the names 
of U.S., British and west German citizens who gave information to 
the communists. The files were used to convict two people in Virginia 
this month.
