Austrian Renate Goetschl won her second women's World Cup downhill 
in as many days Saturday, winning by more than a half second. Goetschl, 
who started 12th, flew down the course in 1 minute, 35.36 seconds. 
Austria apeared headed for a sweep podium sweep at one point on the 
day, but Italy's Isolde Kostner claimed second in 1:35.92 and Germany's 
Regina Haeusl took third in 1:35.95. It was a tough day for the racers, 
who had to endure two lengthy delays due to fog.
