A Vice President of Football's world governing body, says preparations 
in South Korea to co-host the 2002 World Cup tournament are proceeding 
well. After inspecting the site of 64,000-seat stadium, in Seoul on 
Tuesday, FIVA Vice President Antonia Materese said he felt very satisfied 
about the organization. He praised the site extensively, saying he 
expected it to be one of the best stadiums in the world. Groundbreaking 
ceremonies are scheduled for Friday. The FIVA delegation will visit 
co-host Japan, also on Friday. Officials had expressed concern that 
South Korea was lagging far behind Japan in its preparations. And 
that it was time for some of the Korean host cities to begin constructing 
stadiums.
