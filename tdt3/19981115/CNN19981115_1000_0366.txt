Today's "Washington Post" reports district of Columbia police killed 
more people per resident during the 1990s than any other big-city 
force. It found officers fire their weapons twice as often as police 
in New York, los Angeles, Chicago or Miami. 57 people died as a result 
of police shootings over the past five years, including nine unarmed 
civilians who allegedly threatened the officers by driving towards 
them. And D.C. officers were involved in 640 shooting incidents during 
that time. "The post" also uncovered evidence of poor training in 
firearms. 19 officers accidentally shot themselves or other officers.
