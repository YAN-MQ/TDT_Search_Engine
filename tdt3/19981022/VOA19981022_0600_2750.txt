Many years ago, when computers were new and memory was expensive, 
some programmers took a shortcut. Instead of entering the year as 
a four-digit number, they used two. As a result, some older computers 
will function improperly at the end of the century. Beginning Monday, 
the federal government launched what it calls its Year 2000 Y2K Action 
Week. The campaign has many components, including nationwide advertising, 
special education sessions, and even a postage cancellation mark. 
The Information Technology Association of America says that more than 
100 private sector organizations are supporting the government's program.
