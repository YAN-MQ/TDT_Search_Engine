Regional phone companies suffer a legal setback. I'm Jan Hopkins with 
this edition of "Dollars & Sense." A federal appeals court rules that 
current laws governing the entry of local phone companies into the 
long-distance market are constitutional. Those laws require the regional 
bells to open local phone markets to long-distance companies and other 
potential rivals before being granted permission to sell long-distance 
service to local customers. The ruling, a setback for baby bells such 
as bell south, bell atlantic and sbc.
