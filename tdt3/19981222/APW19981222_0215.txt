The United Nations Drug Control Program has granted the government 
of Laos dlrs 900,000 for a program aimed at eradicating opium growing 
and consumption, Laos government radio said on Tuesday. Parts of Laos 
are in the Golden Triangle, the jungle region where the borders of 
Laos, Myanmar and Thailand converge and which is a prime source of 
the world's supply of opium, the raw material for heroin. The report 
said the program will focus on Oudomsay province, the second largest 
opium producing province in the country, and will include aspects 
of community development, crop substitution, income generation, environmental 
management, health and education. It will also include treatment programs 
for addicts. The grant will be distributed over a three-year period.
