Some of the biggest movies of the year are getting the golden nod 
today. The Hollywood foreign press association announced its golden 
globe nominations this morning. They're usually seen as a hint to 
which films might be in line for the academy awards. "Shakespeare 
in love," the story of the bard as a young playwright, tied for most 
nominations with six. They included top musical or comedy, best actress, 
best supporting actor and actress, director and screenwriters. "The 
truman show" also received six nominations, for best actor, best supporting 
actor, director, screenwriter and original score. And the world war 
II epic "saving private Ryan" received five nominations, for dramatic 
film, star Tom Hanks, director Steven Spielberg, screenwriter and 
score.
