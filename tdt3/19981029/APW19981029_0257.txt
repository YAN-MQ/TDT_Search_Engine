Hong Kong share prices were little changed Thursday, after futures 
traders pushed the blue chip index back and forth in moderate trading. 
The Hang Seng Index fell 4.4 points, or 0.04 percent, closing at 9,931.46. 
On Tuesday, the index had risen 148.14 points, or 1.5 percent, to 
9,927.05, its highest close since May 12. Markets were closed Wednesday 
for a public holiday. Turnover rose to 8.59 billion Hong Kong dollars 
(U.S. dlrs 1.1 billion) from Tuesday's 6.03 billion Hong Kong dollars 
(U.S. dlrs 773 million). Among Hong Kong's big real estate companies, 
Sun Hung Kai Properties rose 2.45 Hong Kong dollars to 37.80, while 
Cheung Kong gained 75 cents to 52.25. In the banking sector, market 
heavyweight HSBC Holdings fell 4 dollars to 175.50, while Hang Seng 
Bank lost 1.50 dollars to 65.50. Elsewhere, the conglomerate Swire 
Pacific ``A'' gained 3.20 dollars to 37.80, while Hong Kong's flag 
carrier Cathay Pacific lost 5 cents to 8.20.
