Hurricane Mitch has been downgraded to a category one storm, and is 
almost stationary off the coast of Honduras. Hurricane warnings are 
in effect for the Caribbean coasts of Honduras, guatemala, Belize 
and the Yucatan peninsula of Mexico. Sustained winds from Mitch have 
dropped to 75 miles per hour. The storm is still causing widespread 
flooding in Honduras. An estimated 150,000 residents have evacuated 
low-lying areas, and many people on Honduran islands remain without 
power, communications and drinking water. Flooding is also a concern 
in the Mexican city of chetumal. There are unconfirmed reports of 
three deaths there. So far, 32 deaths have been attributed to the 
hurricane.
