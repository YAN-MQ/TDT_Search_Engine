A federal class-action lawsuit accuses chase Manhattan bank and jp 
Morgan and company of "plundering countless millions of dollars" from 
Jews during world war II. The case, filed yesterday in a New York 
district court, alleges the banks, "Displayed an excessive zeal" in 
enforcing Nazi laws. It accuses the banks of seizing accounts and 
safe deposit boxes from Jewish customers and keeping the assets. The 
suit does not specify damages. Chase and jp Morgan say they are investigating 
the allegations.
