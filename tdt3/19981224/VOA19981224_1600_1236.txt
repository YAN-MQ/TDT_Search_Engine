In U.S. College football, the second of 23 post season bowl games 
was played Wednesday night. The Motor City Bowl featured the thundering 
heard of Marshall taking on the Lewisville Cardinals, Marshall came 
out on top, 48 to 29, in a high scoring contest that featured a couple 
of top quarterbacks. The Marshall's Chad Pennington, threw for 411 
yards and four touchdowns. His team, which had lost only one of 12 
games during the regular season, scored on eight of its final 10 possessions. 
Louisville quarterback, Chris Redman, completed 35 of 54 passes for 
336 yards and one touchdown. Louisville completes its season with 
seven wins and five losses. That is still a huge turnaround from last 
year's record of only one win and ten losses. The U.S. College Football's 
post season play continues Friday with two games in Hawaii, the Aloha 
Bowl and the Oahu Bowl. In the Aloha Bowl, number 21-ranked Oregon 
takes on the Colorado Buffaloes. Oregon quarterback, Achilli Schmidt, 
will try to overcome the Buffaloes' pass defense. Meanwhile, across 
the island in the Oahu Bowl, the Western Athletic Conference Champion, 
Airforce Academy, takes on Washington. The Falcons' defense will try 
to shut down Washington's quarterback, Brock Heulard, who is expected 
to be one of the top picks in the next NFL draft. The bowl season 
continues through January 4 when Florida State and Tennessee will 
play for the National Championship and the Fiesta Bowl.
