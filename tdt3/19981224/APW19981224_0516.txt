An American tourist was stopped at Moscow's international airport 
Thursday for allegedly trying to smuggle dlrs 125,000 in cash out 
of Russia, a news report said. Russian customs officials said the 
money was hidden in the man's luggage, the Interfax news agency reported. 
They said he was a tourist returning from Moscow to New York. No other 
details were immediately available. Also on Thursday, customs officials 
confiscated dlrs 40,000 in undeclared cash from the luggage of a Czech 
Republic national, who was flying to Prague, Interfax reported. Customs 
officials at Sheremetyevo airport also confiscated five bars of gold, 
weighing about 1 kilogram (2.2 pounds), from a Vietnamese citizen, 
the report said. A criminal investigation has been launched in each 
case. Russia's State Customs Committee said Thursday that in the first 
six months of the year, officers confiscated around dlrs 5 million 
and 430 kilograms (946 pounds) of gold from suspected smugglers, Interfax 
reported.
