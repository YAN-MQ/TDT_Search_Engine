The first independent accounts of how many people died when air strikes 
were launched against Iraq are coming in today. The reports are from 
humanitarian workers. The BBC's Caroline Wyatt reports from Baghdad. 
Humanitarian aid workers in Iraq have begun counting the civilian 
cost of last week's air strikes. Michel Minig of the International 
Committee of the Red Cross has given the BBC the first independent 
assessment of civilian casualties. In Baghdad, we have knowledge of 
about 200 wounded. All of them were civilian. We have no knowledge 
of wounded. Despite the bombings and sanctions, the Iraqi government 
remains defiant. In a Christmas message, president Saddam Hussein 
accused Britain and America of continuing what he called their criminal 
aggression against Iraq. Caroline Wyatt in Baghdad.
