Indian and US experts have opened talks in New Delhi on export control 
programs to limit nuclear proliferation. VOA Correspondent Douglas 
Bachshein reports from the Indian capital. US officials say these 
low key talks by technical experts. The American team is lead by Deputy 
Assistant Secretary of State John Barker and includes members of US 
Defense, Commerce, and Energy Departments. The signs are expected 
to study programs for export controls, identify loopholes and how 
to close them, and to try to find a common position on the issue of 
technology exports. The United States has been concerned with the 
spread of nuclear technology in the wake of the nuclear test by India 
and Pakistan in May. The talks come just after President Clinton decided 
to wave some sanctions imposed on the countries because their nuclear 
tests. Washington is pressuring both nations to sign the comprehensive 
test ban treaty. The talks are expected to run one and half days. 
Douglas Backshein, VOA News, New Delhi.
