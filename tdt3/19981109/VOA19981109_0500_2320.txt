Tensions continue to mount in the Indonesian capital one day before 
a government assembly meets to consider political reforms. Hundreds 
of pro and anti-government activist staged demonstrations throughout 
Jukarta which is being controlled by thousands of police and soldiers. 
Pro-government civilians threw stones at the Atmagara Catholic University 
which has become a center of anti-government protest. No one was injured. 
Police did not intervene. One student was injured when police clashed 
with anti-government activist outside the supreme court. Hundreds 
gathered the Jukarta Stock Exchange to protest against Tuesday's opening 
of a special session of the people's consultative assembly.
