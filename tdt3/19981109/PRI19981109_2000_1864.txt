Britain's highest court is still deciding whether or not to extradite 
Chile's Former Military Ruler Augusto Pinochet. Five senior judges 
will decide if they should uphold a previous ruling that General Pinochet 
has immunity. Today, they heard arguments made by General Pinochet's 
lawyers. Barnaby Mason was in court. In a statement over the weekend, 
General Pinochet himself argued that his arrest in hospital have re-opened 
old wounds and damage reconciliation in Chile. At the hearing his 
lawyer, Clare Montgomery, said the five law lords had to consider 
whether the court should interfere with the delicate balance stuck 
in Chile between the interest of justice and state stability. Claire 
Montgomery argued that the question of immunity should be decided 
by the courts there. At least 11 lawsuits have been brought against 
him. Barnaby Mason reporting.
