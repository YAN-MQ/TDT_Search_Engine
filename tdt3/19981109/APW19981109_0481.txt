Two Saudi army helicopters collided and crashed in eastern Saudi Arabia, 
and the four occupants, including one French pilot, parachuted to 
safety, a newspaper reported Monday. The collision occurred Saturday 
in the city of al-Ahsa, 320 kilometers (198 miles) east of Riyadh, 
the Asharq Alawsat newspaper reported. It said the Frenchman, who 
landed near a livestock market, suffered minor neck injuries and was 
taken to a hospital, A defense ministry official said the Frenchman 
was part of a group of French pilots helping to train Saudi pilots. 
No other details were available.
