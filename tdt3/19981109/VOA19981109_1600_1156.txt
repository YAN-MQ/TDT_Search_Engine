The National Football League's Pittsburgh Steelers held on to beat 
the Green Bay Packers 27 to 20 in Monday night action. The Steelers 
jumped to a huge 24 nothing half-time lead behind quarterback Cordell 
Stewart's four scoring drives. Stewart finished the game 231 yards 
passing and 41 yards rushing, including a touchdown from the one yard 
line. The victory improves the Steelers to six and three, keeping 
them in second place in the AFC Central Division one game behind Jacksonville. 
The loss drops the Packers to six and three, two games behind the 
NFC Central leading Minnesota Vikings.
