The European Commission, 20-member executive body of the European 
Union, on Thursday announced the irrevocable conversion rates between 
the euro and currencies from the 11 nations participating in the shared 
currency. EU finance ministers were to give final approval to the 
rates later in the day. From its official launch at midnight Thursday, 
the euro will be worth: 1.95583 German marks. 6.55957 French francs. 
1936.27 Italian lire. 166.386 Spanish pesetas. 2.20371 Dutch guilders. 
40.3399 Belgian francs. 13.7603 Austrian schillings. 200.482 Portuguese 
escudos. 5.94573 Finnish markka. 0.787564 Irish pounds. 40.3399 Luxembourg 
francs. Based on those rates, the euro expected to start trading at 
dlrs 1.1665.
