Mexico's President Ernesto Zedillo has called on President Clinton 
to discuss details of the U.S. leader's upcoming visit to Mexico. 
Mr. Clinton and Mr. Zedillo plan to meet February 14th and 15th in 
the southeastern city of Merida. A White House spokesman says the 
two presidents exchanged New Year's greetings and discussed ongoing 
relief efforts for Central American countries ravaged by Hurricane 
Mitch. Mexico will be President Clinton's last stop on a tour of Central 
America. The White House says his itinerary has not been finalized, 
but he plans to visit Honduras and may also stop in Nicaragua, Guatemala, 
and El Salvador.
