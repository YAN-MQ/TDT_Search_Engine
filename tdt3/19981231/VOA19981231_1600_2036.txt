Christian groups in India have welcomed Prime Minister Atal Bihari 
Vadjpayee promise to safeguard their safety saying it will instill 
confidence among Christians. Mr. Vajpayee has condemned attacks by 
Hindu radicals on Christian churches and has asked the government 
in the worst affected state of Gudjarat to take stern action against 
those responsible. Meanwhile, more attacks on Christians are being 
reported as Leia Terhoun reports from New Delhi, four nuns and two 
priests are said to be injured. Assaults on Christians in the Indian 
state of Gudjarat continued Wednesday as a mob attacked a Christian 
church injuring several nuns and priests. Officials say the attackers 
set the church on fire with the injured inside. No one was seriously 
hurt. A series of violent acts against Christians has intensified 
since Christmas Day when several churches in the same area were burned. 
The victims accuse extremist Hindu groups who justify and sometimes 
promote violence against Christians. The extremists say Christians 
force conversion. Christians strongly deny the Hindu charges. Christian 
activist say attacks have skyrocketed in India since 1997. The United 
Christian Forum for Human Rights cites more than 90 this year, including 
rapes and murders of nuns and priests. This is Leia Terhoun for VOA 
News, New Delhi.
