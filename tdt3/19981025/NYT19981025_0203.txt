James V. Hallisey, a lawyer specializing in health care and Surrogate's 
Court matters, died on Oct. 16 at his home in Manhattan. He was 70. 
The cause was cancer, said his daughter, Tamara Hallisey-Hatfield. 
Hallisey, a Manhattan lawyer for the last 32 years, was a legal consultant 
to the Catholic Charities of the Archdiocese of New York and was on 
the boards of the Catholic Youth Organization, the Catholic Health 
Care Network and Our Lady of Mercy Health Care System, which includes 
nursing homes in New York City. He was also chairman of Rosalie Hall, 
a home for unwed mothers in the Bronx, and he was active in the affairs 
of Carmel Richmond Nursing Home on Staten Island. Hallisey was born 
and raised in Hell's Kitchen. He served in the Marines during the 
Korean War and graduated in 1954 from Iona College and in 1957 from 
the Fordham University Law School. In 1956, he married Nancy Spafford, 
who died in 1988. Besides his daughter, of Manhattan, he is survived 
by a son, James Hallisey Jr. of Manhattan; two grandchildren; a brother; 
and two sisters.
