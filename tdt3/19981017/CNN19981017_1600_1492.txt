A Jesuit brother working with impoverished children in Peru has found 
a Creative way to raise money. At a school outside Lima, 1300 students 
learn practical trades, like working with wood, ceramics and clothes. 
Brother Paul macauley has persuaded the harrods department store in 
London to sell their products. Harrods owner, Mohammed Al-Fayed, visited 
Peru last month. Macauley says interest in the products his students 
are making has grown tremendously.
