A ferry company pleaded guilty to discharging oil into Red Hook Bay 
on St. Thomas and will pay a dlrs 50,000 fine and spend dlrs 300,000 
upgrading its equipment as part of a plea bargain, federal prosecutors 
said Friday. U.S. Attorney James Hurd said Varlack Ventures, Inc., 
the company that was operating the ferry M/V Venture Pride when the 
oil discharge occurred on March 26, 1995, will also publish an apology 
to the community in local newspapers. Hurd said the company is required 
to spend dlrs 300,000 beyond its normal maintaince costs to upgrade 
its vessels and to modernize its oil-handling and waste oil disposal 
systems.
