The United States, Austria and Ukraine all assured their place in 
the Pool A World Ice Hockey Championship on Saturday, winning to clinch 
three of four spots in next year's tournament. Corey Millen scored 
three times to lead the United States to a 7-1 victory over Estonia; 
Dieter Kalt had two goals and an assist to give Austria a 6-2 victory 
over Kazakstan 6-2; and Oleksandr Zineyvich and Dmitri Markovsky scored 
to lift Ukraine over Germany 2-1. The victories left all three 2-0-0 
records and four points with one game remaining each. The United States 
faces Austria on Sunday. Estonia and Kazakstan, playing in the one 
of two qualifying groups with the Americans and Austrians, both dropped 
to 0-0-2 in Group B will fall to the Pool B championship. Ukraine 
moved two points ahead of France and three ahead of both Slovenia 
and Germany. France beat the Slovenes 5-2 in the only other game Saturday. 
The results mean France heads into the final day of play with the 
advantage over the Germans and Slovenes for the final remaining berth 
in the Pool A championship. The French play Germany while Slovenia 
and Ukraine on Sunday.
