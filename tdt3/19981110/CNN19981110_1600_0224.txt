A bipartisan group of health experts is calling for the expansion 
of "drug courts." First used in dade county, Florida, the courts allow 
non-violent drug offenders to go into rehabilitation rather than serve 
time. A new study shows they can help reduce drug use and criminal 
behavior. The White House wants to triple the number of drug court 
programs, which now total about 300 nationwide.
