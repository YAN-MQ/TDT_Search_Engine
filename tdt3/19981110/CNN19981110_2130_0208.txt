Sources tell CNN, a settlement between the tobacco industry and eight 
state attorneys general is near. The states are seeking to recover 
the cost of smoking-related medical expenses. Two of the attorneys 
general involved tell CNN the deal would mean paying the states about 
$200 billion over 25 years. The settlement likely will be made public 
later this week.
