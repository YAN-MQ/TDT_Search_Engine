Talking Turkey Toll-free hot lines are sizzling with questions about 
how to roast, when to baste and what to bake. The U.S. Agriculture 
Department, (800) 535-4555, has recorded messages around the clock, 
with operators on hand weekdays from 10 a.m. to 4 p.m; its Web site 
is at www.foodsafety.org. Shady Brook Farms offers recorded advice 
from chefs through Jan. 1, at (888) 723-4468, and on the Internet 
at www.dialachef.com. The Butterball Turkey Talk-Line, (800) 323-4848, 
which has operators on hand and also promotes some brand-name products, 
is available through Dec. 23, 9 a.m. to 9 p.m., and on Thanksgiving 
Day from 7 a.m. to 8 p.m.; its Web site is at www.butterball.com. 
The Land O'Lakes Holiday Bakeline, (800) 782-9606, has operators on 
hand from 9 a.m. to 7 p.m. through Dec. 24; its Web address is www.landolakes.com. 
And the Campbell's Soup Holiday Leftover Hotline will be at its best 
on Nov. 27 from 8 a.m. to 8 p.m., when there will be operators, not 
just recordings (as there are through Dec. 31): (888) 453-3868 and 
www.campbellsoup.com. Seasonal cooking advice is also given on the 
Internet by a diverse group of chefs and food experts, including Sylvia 
Woods, an owner of Sylvia's in Harlem, and Pamela Morgan, the owner 
of Flavors Catering. Click on ``Turkey Tips'' at www.starchef.com.
