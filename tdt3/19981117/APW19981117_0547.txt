Fire broke out Tuesday night at a nine-story hotel in western Japan 
and spread to its five-story annex and parts of a nearby forest, but 
all 51 guests were evacuated safely, police said. There were no reports 
of injuries among guests or workers at the Hotel Tenzankaku, said 
Masanori Ueno, a spokesman of police in Shirahama, a resort about 
450 kilometers (280 miles) southwest of Tokyo. The fire broke out 
at 6:38 p.m. (0938 GMT) and still was burning four hours later, with 
30 fire engines fighting the flames, Ueno said. The cause of the fire 
was not immediately known, he added. Kyodo News agency said the flames, 
fanned by strong winds, had reached the roof of the hotel. The 37-year-old 
Hotel Tenzankaku has 93 rooms, capable of accommodating about 450 
people.
