Monica Seles of the United States defeated Japan's Naoko Sawamatsu 
on Friday in the opening match of the Sony-Life Cup, giving the U.S. 
team a 1-0 lead in the three-day event. Seles, ranked No. 6 in the 
world, beat Sawamatsu 6-0, 3-6, 6-0 at the Hiroshima Green Arena on 
the first day of the all-women tournament. Sawamatsu, who now has 
an 0-11 career record versus Seles, has said she plans to retire after 
this tournament. In the event, a team from the United States competes 
against a Japanese team in five singles and two doubles matches. The 
winner takes home dlrs 250,000.
