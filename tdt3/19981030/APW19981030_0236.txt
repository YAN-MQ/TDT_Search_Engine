Two undersea earthquakes rocked southeastern parts of Indonesia early 
Friday, but there were no immediate reports of damage or casualties. 
The first quake, with a preliminary magnitude of 5.5, struck at 1:08 
a.m. (1608 GMT Thursday), and was centered on Maluku Sea, about 165 
kilometers southeast of Bitung on Sulawesi Island, the Meteorology 
and Geophysics Agency said. The second, magnitude 5.2 earthquake came 
five hours later, also centered on the Maluku Sea, about 140 kilometers 
87 miles) southwest of Ternate, an island in northern Maluku. The 
quakes were the latest in a series shaking the region since last Saturday. 
An earthquake of magnitude 5 can cause considerable damage in populated 
areas.
