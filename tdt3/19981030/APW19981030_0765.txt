Ferrari supporters were preparing for a nationwide overnight vigil 
to follow the final World Championship race, the Grand Prix of Japan, 
on television screens Sunday. In Maranello, the hometown of the idolized 
Italian Formula One team, thousands will watch the race on a huge 
outdoor screen near the company headquarters. The race, featuring 
a decisive duel for the world drivers title between Ferrari's Michael 
Schumacher and McLaren's Mika Hakkinen, is scheduled to start at 0400gmt. 
Organizers in Maranello said they expected fans by the thousands and 
that, barring rain, food, drinks and entertaintment were planned to 
keep people lively and awake. In occasion of last year's decisive 
Grand Prix in Yerez, in which Schumacher lost the title to Jacques 
Villeneuve, about 15,000 followed the race in an outdoor area at Maranello. 
Schumacher enters Sunday's decisive championship race four points 
behind Hakkinen.
