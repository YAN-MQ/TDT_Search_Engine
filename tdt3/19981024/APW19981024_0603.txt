Brazilian senator Vilson Kleinubing has died of cancer, news media 
reported Saturday. He was 54. Kleinubing died Friday. His funeral 
took place Saturday with military honors, reported TV Globo. Kleinubing, 
a member of the Liberal Front Party, has represented for the southeastern 
state of Santa Catarina since 1995. In the 1980s, Kleinubing served 
as the state's secretary for agriculture and provisions. ``Brazil 
has lost a great public figure,'' Senate President Antonio Carlos 
Magalhaes told the O Folha De Sao Paulo newspaper Saturday. He is 
survived by a wife and three sons.
