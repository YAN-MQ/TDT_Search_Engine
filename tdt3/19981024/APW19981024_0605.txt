Hundreds of thousands of Italians supporting media mogul Silvio Berlusconi's 
center-right political alliance marched in Rome Saturday to protest 
the formation of Massimo D'Alema's new government with the help of 
turncoat lawmakers. D'Alema, a former Communist, cemented his majority 
earlier in the week by including centrists who were elected to Parliament 
in 1996 under Berlusconi's Freedom Alliance ticket. Several lines 
of marchers converged on St. John in Lateran square in Rome for an 
evening rally by Berlusconi and other opposition figures, including 
his chief ally, Gianfranco Fini, who leads the rightwing National 
Alliance party. Fini called the mass turnout ``confirmation of a great 
indignation in the country but also of a great will to change. There's 
hope, not just anger.'' Rome's police headquarters said that a final 
estimate of crowd hadn't been made yet since people were still streaming 
to the square. But it appeared a few hundred thousand people were 
participating, police added. A National Alliance deputy suffered a 
mouth injury when security personnel for Fini's and Berlusconi's parties
