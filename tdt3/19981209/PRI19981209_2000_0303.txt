The gap between what the U.S. exports and what Americans buy from 
abroad grew to a record of more than $61 billion in the third quarter. 
The Commerce Department said the current account deficit grew by more 
than 8 percent from July through September. It was the fourth record 
trade deficit in a row. Exports by American manufacturers and farmers 
have been hurt by the Asian financial crisis and by competition from 
imported goods made cheaper by the devaluation of their home currencies.
