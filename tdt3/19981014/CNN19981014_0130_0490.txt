Five scientists at universities in the United States have won this 
year's nobel prizes in chemistry and physics. The chemistry award 
goes to Austrian Walter kohn of University of California at Santa 
Barbara for developing the density-functional theory. He shares the 
award with British national John pople of northwestern University 
who developed computational methods in Quantum chemistry. The nobel 
winners in physics are Robert laughlin, horst stoermer and Daniel 
tsui . The professors are cited for discovering how electrons can 
change their behavior.
