Israel's Parliament has voted overwhelmingly for early elections, 
rejecting Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu's peace policies. The 
vote, 81-30 with four abstentions came after Parliament rejected Mr. 
Netanyahu's conditions for resuming the peace process with the Palestinians. 
And after the opposition rejected Mr. Netanyahu's conditions for resuming 
the peace process with the Palestinians. And after the opposition 
rejected Mr. Netanyahu's appeal to form a unity government. By voting 
for early elections, the Knesset put aside a vote on a no confidence 
motion that would have forced new elections with sixty days. New elections 
could be held in April 18 months ahead of schedule.
