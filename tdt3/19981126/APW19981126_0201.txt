The International Herald Tribune announced Thursday that it has begun 
printing in Jakarta, Indonesia, the daily's 17th remote printsite. 
The IHT will be printed on the P.T. Gramedia presses and expects to 
build its circulation in Indonesia through morning delivery to homes 
and offices, the company said. Founded in Paris in 1887, the International 
Herald Tribune is jointly owned by the New York Times and the Washington 
Post. The paper is on sale in more than 180 countries.
