Christoph Langen of Germany won his third straight bobsled World Cup 
victory Friday, capturing a two-man race with new brakeman Marco Jakobs. 
Langen, the four-man Olympic champion, set a track record of 55.68 
seconds in the first heat, beating his two-year-old mark by .63 seconds. 
In the second heat, Langen again guided his bobsled to the fastest 
time, clocking 55.88 for a winning total of 1:51.56. The Germans were 
.40 seconds faster than Swiss world champion Reto Goetschi and brakeman 
Guido Acklin. The third place went to Olympic and World Cup two-man 
defending champion Pierre Lueders of Canada, with brakeman Ken LeBlanc. 
Langen opened an eight-point lead in World Cup standings with 106 
points. Lueders is second with 98, followed by Goetschi with 90.
