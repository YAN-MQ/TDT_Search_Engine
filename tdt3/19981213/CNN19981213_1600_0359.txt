Puerto Ricans cast votes today, deciding whether to become the 51st 
state of the U.S. the island is currently an American commonwealth 
in which residents have U.S. citizenship but no voting power. Election 
officials report a high turnout among the 2.2 million registered voters. 
The ballot initiative included several options, Puerto Rico could 
remain a Commonwealth. It could push the U.S. Congress for statehood 
or seek independence. The last time Puerto Rico voted for statehood 
in 1993, it lost by 2 percentage points.
