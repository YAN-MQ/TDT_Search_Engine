Low turnout marked the second round of legislative elections Sunday 
in the Central African Republic, where the ruling party and the opposition 
are vying for control of parliament. International observers, speaking 
on condition of anonymity, estimated the turnout at 35-40 percent 
just before polling ended at at 6 p.m. It was not clear when results 
would be released. The run-up to the election has been marred by violence, 
including a Wednesday clash between supporters of the ruling party 
and the opposition that left six people wounded in Bangui, the capital. 
Fearing more violence, government soldiers and U.N. peacekeepers patrolled 
the city and polling places throughout the country, but there were 
no immediate reports of disturbances. Fourteen political parties, 
many of them set up along ethnic lines, are vying for the 63 seats 
that were not captured in the first round of voting on Nov. 22. Fifty-five 
seats are needed to take control of 109-seat parliament. President 
Ange-Felix Patasse's ruling Movement for the Liberation of the People 
of Centrafrique, or MLPC, won 26 slots in the first round and opposition
